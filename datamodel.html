Statistical Modeling


The hypothesis states that "The tweets were made strategically at dates where election-related news/events were happening."

In order to prove the hypothesis, the data scientists needed to determine whether the number of tweets have any relationship with the date when the tweets were made.

By identifying the variables in the dataset, the scientists chose the most appropriate statistical model as guided by a Scribbr flowchart. The predictor variable is the date which is quantitative in nature and the target variable is the number of tweets which is also quantitative in nature. The scientists utilized the Linear Regression Model in order to determine if the number of tweets have any relationship with the date posted.

These are the returned results of the model:
   - RMSE = 3.89
   - R2 = 0.29
   - P(x1): 0.0033617160347947355
   - There is a significant relationship between the predictor and response

The RMSE tells us the average difference between the actual and predicted values. The R2 tells us us how well the regression line approximates the actual data. These two aren't really significant in this context since we aren't trying to predict the values but rather determine the relationship between variables.
The p-value, whose value is significantly below the 0.05 threshold, is the most important component of the outcome. This indicates that there is a strong correlation between the quantity of tweets and the posting date.
